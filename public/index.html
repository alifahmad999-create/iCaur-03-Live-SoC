Release Notes
v10.4
Fixed: long legs lost the start of their route. The live GPS buffer used to discard its oldest samples once full (~66 min), so any leg longer than that lost the beginning of its trace — road-trip share cards showed fragmented routes with blank stretches, and because duration was summed from that same trimmed data, avg speed read impossibly high (distance was right, time was short). The buffer now thins itself evenly instead: resolution steps down gently on very long drives, but the trace and duration always cover the whole leg. Genuine GPS outages (tunnels, locked phone) now render as an honest gap in the line rather than a fake straight road.
Road trips can now hold 4 legs (was 3). Combine up to four trips in History into one road trip — e.g. a long day with two charge stops. The selection bar shows a 0/4 counter and flags when the max is reached; the combined map, ⚡ charge nodes and summed stats all handle the fourth leg automatically.
Speed tables extended to 160 km/h. The Speed Scenarios table and the Insights chart now run 140 / 150 / 160 rows, and every vehicle variant's consumption curve extends to 160 — the 03 iWD from real head-unit data, the others estimated on the same aero slope. GPS distance sampling also accepts real top-speed pulls (up to ~170 implied) instead of dropping them as glitches.
Verify SOC card cleanup. The label is shortened so it never clips on the small card, and you can now reliably type the head-unit % — tapping the value freezes the live tick and selects it, so your first keystroke replaces it instead of fighting the updating number. Nudge buttons unchanged.
Fixed: arrival estimate stuck @ 0 km/h. After a trip, the speed field could silently stay at 0 (left behind by GPS), so the next pre-trip Estimated Arrival SOC was computed at 0 km/h. Stopping a trip now restores the calibrated 110 km/h planning default, with a safety net that snaps any stale pre-trip 0 back to 110.
v10.3
Verify Live SOC while you drive. During a trip, the Reserve Target card becomes a live, editable Verify Live SOC value, with a control bar below the inputs. Glance at your head unit, type or nudge (−2 / −1 / +1 / +2) the % it shows, and tap Match head unit. The app snaps the live SOC to that value and — crucially — learns from the difference, so the Estimated Arrival SOC gets more accurate every trip and you'll need to correct it less and less. The card reverts to the fixed Reserve Target once the trip ends.
Hero back to its clean look. The tap-to-reveal low-SOC message under the hero has been removed, restoring the original uncluttered hero. Low-arrival cues still show as the tier colour (green / amber / red) on the hero ring and the below-reserve note.
v10.2
Predicted vs actual on the share card. The big headline is now your actual Arrival SOC — the figure from the head unit you calibrate with. Underneath, in a smaller italic, sits the app's predicted arrival (the live Estimated Arrival SOC as it stood the moment you stopped). So the card tells the whole story at a glance: "predicted X%, arrived at Y%." Display-only — it never touches calibration or the live SOC model.
v10.1 MAJOR
Premium share card. A full redesign built for sharing: the route trace is the hero, drawn as a glowing drain-tinted line with a legend so anyone understands the colours. The headline is your tier-coloured Estimated Arrival SOC (green comfortable, amber tight, red cutting it close — matching the app), with the pre-trip predicted value shown small beside it.
Right map for the trip. Short within-state drives (under 40 km) now show a clean route-only trace so the actual turns are visible, instead of a meaningless magnified coastline. Longer interstate trips keep the recognisable Peninsular Malaysia map.
Six stats, in the car's own language. A clean 2×3 grid: Distance, Avg kWh/100km, Energy used, Avg speed, CO₂ saved (vs petrol), and Duration. Everything is in kWh and kWh/100km — no Wh/km, since the head unit never shows that.
Combined trips tell the whole story. A road trip's legs are unioned onto one map, and an amber ⚡ node marks each charge stop, so the energy numbers make sense at a glance (drove → charged → drove). Distance, energy and duration sum across legs; avg speed and kWh/100km are recomputed from the totals.
No more overlapping text. The card lays out in fixed bands, so a long trip name or wide values can never collide with the stats or footer. Un-renamed trip titles auto-shrink to fit. Footer credits you and Claude.
v10 MAJOR
Final accuracy pass — the live card and arrival estimate now agree at the source. The arrival prediction reads your true live battery level (full precision) instead of the rounded number shown in the box, so the headline and the live card no longer drift apart from rounding alone. Combined with the changes below, predictions should sit within ~1% of your head unit and let calibration quietly close the rest.
Full-charge "phantom 1%" solved. After charging to 100% the car holds 100% for hours, then snaps to 99% the moment you accelerate — that's the battery's top buffer releasing on first real load, not a kilometre of driving. The live card now expects this one-time ~1% drop on trips that start at 99–100%, and the arrival estimate anticipates it from the very start so it's never briefly optimistic. Trips starting at any other level are untouched. Crucially, this phantom drop is excluded from calibration, so a full-charge trip never inflates your learned factor with energy you didn't actually spend driving.
Smarter near-destination lock. The arrival estimate can no longer read lower than your live SOC when you're almost there. Instead of a fixed distance, it now keys off how much charge the road ahead is actually worth — so it settles onto the live card at about 2 km on an open highway but waits longer in stop-go traffic, matching reality either way.
Share-card map zoom — corrected. Short local trips (under 25 km) now zoom in and centre on your route so the drive is actually visible, while the map outline is clamped so it can never break out of the frame and leave gaps. Long trips (25 km and up) keep the whole recognisable Peninsular Malaysia view. → Updated in v10.1: the threshold moved to 40 km and short trips now show a route-only trace.
Calibration pill protected. A stray tap used to open the reset prompt and could wipe your hard-earned calibration. Resetting now needs a deliberate press-and-hold.
Live-card calibration visible. Settings now shows the live-card calibration factors, so you can watch the card converge on your head unit over a few trips.
Removed a redundant "AUTO" tag from the average-speed card — the GPS pill already shows when speed has gone live.
v9.7
The share-card route line now colours by how hard the car was actually drawing power, not just by the slope of the road. Before, a flat highway pull always read blue no matter how fast you were going — the line only knew about hills. Now it blends speed and grade into a real Wh/km estimate, so a hard fast stretch warms toward amber/red and an easy or regen stretch cools to teal. The line finally matches its own "Efficient → Highest Drain" legend.
You can now name individual trips, not just road-trip groups. Tap the pencil next to any trip's date in History to give it a name like "Morning commute" — it shows in the list and becomes the title on the share card. Leave it blank to go back to the date and the default "iCaur trip" label.
The base consumption curve was re-anchored to your head unit's real numbers (lifetime 13.4 / mixed-trip 14.6 kWh per 100km), so the live SOC card reads true without leaning on calibration. High-speed figures were kept realistic since aero drag dominates above 110. → Built on in v10: the live card and arrival estimate now read the same full-precision battery level at the source.
The live SOC card now quietly learns. After each trip you calibrate, it compares how much battery it expected to use against what the car really used, and nudges its own reading closer for next time — separate from the arrival prediction's tuning, and weather-free since the card already sees live conditions. It works in the background with no settings to touch. Tip: after this update, reset calibration once so both the arrival and live-card learning start fresh from the new curve.
The share-card map now zooms to fit the trip. Short drives like a KL–Seremban run used to show as a tiny line lost on the whole peninsula; the map now scales in and centres on the route, keeping enough surrounding coastline to stay recognisable. Long trips still show the wider map. → Refined in v10 & v10.1: the short-trip threshold and route-only view changed — see those entries for current behaviour.
Your battery level is now kept at the end of every trip, even if you skip calibration — so a refresh or your next session starts from where you actually finished. Confirming your real SOC still overrides it with the exact head-unit value.
v9.6
Brand-new share card map: the wobbly hand-traced peninsula is gone, replaced by a clean, accurate map of Peninsular Malaysia with proper state borders, Penang, Langkawi and the east-coast islands — themed to match the card. Your route glows on top of real geography now.
Calibration now learns separately for short trips and long trips. Short city runs (under 15km) spend most of their energy on fixed overhead — AC, electronics, cabin draw — spread over little distance, so their consumption looks much higher than a long highway leg. Lumping them together was inflating the factor and hurting both. Now each distance band keeps its own factor: short trips converge fast and tight, long trips learn slowly.
Long-trip calibration freezes once it's stable: after a few closely-matching long drives, the factor locks (shown with 🔒) and stops chasing normal weather and terrain wobble. It only resumes adjusting if a trip disagrees enough to matter. Short trips always keep learning.
The arrival prediction picks the right band automatically from the distance still ahead, so a short remaining hop and a long haul each use the factor that fits. The calibration pill and Insights now show both factors so you can watch them diverge.
v9.1
During a trip, tap the SOC card to switch it between battery % and total range left (km) — Tesla-style. The range uses your live consumption, so it's a real-world figure, not an optimistic spec number. The card re-themes and keeps the same size. Before a trip the card is just for entering your SOC.
The "tuned from N similar trips" note on the arrival prediction now shows as a clearer accent badge — only when there's enough history and a distance left to predict.
v9.0 MAJOR
Context-aware learning: the arrival prediction now learns from your own history. As you drive, it finds past trips in similar conditions — the same kind of weather (wet/dry, wind, heat) on the same road direction — and tunes the estimate from how those actually turned out. So a rainy run on a familiar route predicts lower automatically. Shows "tuned from N similar trips" when active, and quietly falls back to the standard model when there isn’t enough similar history, so it only ever gets better as you log more drives.
Hot-weather handling tuned for Malaysia: a normal ~32°C day is treated as baseline, and only hotter days add a small AC nudge. Because temperature is part of each trip’s fingerprint, the app learns your real hot-day consumption — reflecting how you actually run the AC — rather than assuming a heavy generic load.
Polish & hardening: combined-weather effects are now capped so storms can’t over-penalise the estimate; learning requires a real route match (never weather alone); and the last few native pop-ups were replaced with the app’s dark-themed dialogs for a consistent look.
